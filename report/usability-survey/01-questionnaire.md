# Paper Trail — Usability Survey Questionnaire

This questionnaire was given to **12 participants** (target was ≥ 10) after walking them through the low-fidelity prototype (`../low-fidelity-prototype/`). Participants were a mix of senior CS undergraduates, MS thesis students, and one faculty member — i.e. the real target audience.

---

## Section A — Demographics

| # | Question | Type |
|---|---|---|
| A1 | What is your role? | Single-choice: Undergraduate / MS Student / PhD Student / Faculty / Industry Researcher |
| A2 | How often do you read academic papers? | Single-choice: Daily / Few times a week / Weekly / Monthly / Rarely |
| A3 | What tools do you currently use to manage references? | Multi-choice: Zotero / Mendeley / EndNote / Google Scholar / None / Other |
| A4 | Have you used an AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) to help with research before? | Yes / No |

---

## Section B — Ease of Use (Closed-ended, 1–5 Likert)

> *1 = Strongly Disagree, 5 = Strongly Agree*

| # | Question |
|---|---|
| B1 | The purpose of the application was clear from the landing page. |
| B2 | I could find where to upload a document without guidance. |
| B3 | The dashboard surfaces information that is relevant to me. |
| B4 | The location of the AI chat panel (right of the document) feels natural. |
| B5 | Multi-document tabs above the viewer help me work with several papers at once. |
| B6 | Citation cards make it obvious where the AI's answer came from. |

---

## Section C — Navigation (Closed-ended, 1–5 Likert)

| # | Question |
|---|---|

## Administration notes

- Estimated completion time: **8–12 minutes**.
- Conducted in person + via Google Forms (mix of synchronous walk-throughs and async).
- Participants were shown each low-fi screen for ~30 seconds before answering its block.
